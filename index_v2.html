gavel Informed Consent

Treatment To Be Done

I consent to any treatment performed by the dentist after the procedure, risks, benefits, and cost have been explained — including x-rays, cleanings, periodontal treatment, fillings, crowns, bridges, extractions, root canals, dentures, local anesthesia, and surgical cases.

Drugs & Medications

Antibiotics, analgesics, and other medications can occasionally cause allergic reactions such as redness, swelling, itching, vomiting, or anaphylactic shock.

Changes In Treatment Plan

During treatment it may be necessary to change or add procedures for conditions found while working — for example, an unplanned root canal — and I accept responsibility for the added cost.

Radiograph

X-rays help form a treatment plan but do not guarantee 100% accuracy; unpredictable complications may still change the plan later.

Removal of Teeth

Alternatives to extraction and their risks have been explained. Removing teeth does not always clear existing infection, and further treatment, pain, swelling, dry socket, or numbness of teeth/lips/tongue may follow.

Crowns, Bridges & Endodontics

Tooth preparation can irritate the nerve; temporary crowns must be kept on and permanent cementation returned to promptly. Root canal treatment has no guarantee of success and a tooth may still need removal despite best efforts.

Periodontal Disease, Fillings & Dentures

Gum disease can lead to tooth loss if untreated. Fillings may be sensitive at first. Dentures can be difficult to adjust to and may require several relines; missed delivery appointments may affect fit.

I understand that dentistry is not an exact science and that no dentist can properly guarantee accurate results all the time. I authorize the clinic's dentists and staff to proceed with the dental restorations and treatments explained to me, and I am responsible for payment of dental fees regardless of insurance coverage.
